Hi! My name is Jalyn Vo and I am a pre-medical student. I am a recent graduate from the University of California, Riverside and attained a Bachelors of Science in Biology. I anticipate taking 1 or 2 gap years before applying to medical school. Currently, I am working towards attending a masters program in Biomedical Sciences to further prepare me for the rigors of medical school. This coming year, I plan to gain more clinical experience to further my knowledge and expand my capabilities in medicine and healthcare.

As I am applying to masters programs, I do prefer to attend schools that have a medical program or linkage by interview or conditional acceptance. By my proven upward trend and continuous streak of all A's for the past two years, I have demonstrated not only the academic understanding that is necessary to excel in medical school classes, but perseverence as grit is an essential character that students need to instill. I am looking for a 9-12 month program where the faculty to student ratio is small so I can ensure I will be able to receive the support and aide to be successful in the program and acquire the neccessary skillsets to be a thorough and empathetic physician in the future.

In order to attain more clinical hours, I am searching for a scribe position or medical assistant position that is part time. For scribing I prefer that it is a position in the Emergency Room to examine a wide range of cases and have the most patient interaction. I am flexible around hours as I know the ER operates 24/7 in comparison to a more calm environment in clinic settings. However, I am eager to adapt and learn from not only doctors, but all medical staff to understand how all of the patient care team functions to execute the best treatment and response towards patients. From this position, I will also be able to enhance my skillsets working in a team and foster professional yet compassionate relationships with patients and their loved ones. For a medical assistant position, I have a clinical focus so I want to deter from administrative work. From my experience in clerical work from being a front desk receptionist and back office assistant at the Radiation Oncology clinic at Riverside Community Hospital, my most meaningful experiences were from interacting with patients and their families, taking vitals, and presenting a history and physical.
